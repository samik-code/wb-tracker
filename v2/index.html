A Note on Good Faith

This tracker was created with genuine good faith — we believe in holding elected governments accountable to the promises they made publicly, and we want this BJP government to succeed in fulfilling them for the people of West Bengal. We are not here to root for failure.

We are ordinary people, not journalists. There is a real possibility that we miss news, fail to update things quickly, or are unaware of developments on the ground. That is exactly why we need your help. If you know that something has been done, started, or abandoned — please tell us.

Methodology & Standards

01
Source of promises
All promises are sourced verbatim from the official BJP West Bengal manifesto, Bhoroshar Shopoth, released ahead of the 2026 Assembly Election.
📄 Read the official manifesto →
02
Source hierarchy for fulfilment
We follow a strict source hierarchy: (1) Official government gazette or government order — the gold standard. (2) State government press release or official cabinet statement. (3) PTI / ANI wire reports carried by national publications like Business Standard, The Hindu, Indian Express, or Hindustan Times. Quotes from party politicians or social media posts do not qualify as sources.
03
How a promise is marked "In Progress"
A promise is marked in progress when any credible news report, cabinet statement, or official announcement confirms the government has initiated or is actively pursuing it — with a source link attached. A formal gazette or tender is not required for "In Progress".
04
What "Evaded" means
A promise is marked evaded when the government has taken action that makes its fulfilment structurally impossible — for example, privatising an industry that was promised revival, or repealing a scheme it pledged to implement. Pending implies it could still happen. Evaded means the door has been closed.
05
How a promise is marked "Fulfilled"
Fulfilled requires a formal document (gazette notification, bill enacted, official order) or verified on-ground evidence that beneficiaries are receiving the benefit — sourced from a Tier 1 or 2 publication.
06
Term tracked
Oath ceremony: 9 May 2026. All promises are tracked over the full 5-year term ending May 2031.
